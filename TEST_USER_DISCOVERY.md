# Test User Discovery Guide — Mobile Provider

**Who:** your contact who currently does mobile hair services.
**Goal:** understand their real workflow and pains so we build the right features (and price it
right) — and recruit them as our first live profile.
**How to use:** keep it a relaxed conversation, not an interrogation. Aim ~30–45 min. Let them
talk; the "why" behind each answer matters more than yes/no. Take notes against each section.

> Framing for them: "We're building a tool to make running your mobile business easier — bookings,
> reminders, payments, a professional profile — for a small flat fee, where you keep everything you
> earn and your clients stay yours. I want to understand how you work today so we build what
> actually helps."

---

## 1. Their work today
- What services do you offer, and what do they cost?
- How many clients do you see in a typical week? Busy vs quiet periods?
- Do you work only mobile (travel to clients), or also from home / a chair somewhere?

## 2. How clients find & book them
- How do new clients find you today? (Word of mouth, Instagram, WhatsApp, Gumtree, Home Salon…?)
- How do they book — WhatsApp, calls, DMs? Walk me through a typical booking.
- Roughly what share are repeat clients vs new?
- Do you use any app or site now (Home Salon, Fresha, anything)? What do you like / dislike?

## 3. Scheduling & travel
- How do you keep track of your appointments? (Phone calendar, notebook, memory?)
- How do you decide if you can fit someone in / how far you'll travel?
- Have you ever double-booked or run late because of travel? How often?
- What areas do you cover? Is there a distance you won't go past?

## 4. Payments & deposits
- How do clients pay you — cash, card, EFT, SnapScan/Zapper, other?
- Do you ever ask for a deposit to secure a booking? Why / why not?
- Have you been left out of pocket (client cancels last-minute, no-show, doesn't pay)?

## 5. No-shows & cancellations
- How often do clients no-show or cancel late? What does that cost you?
- What do you do about it now (if anything)?
- Would automatic reminders the day before help? Would you want them by WhatsApp, SMS, or email?

## 6. Reputation & trust
- How do you build trust with a new client who's never met you?
- Do clients ever ask to see photos of your work, or reviews?
- How would you feel about a public profile with your photo, ratings, and a count of returning
  clients? Helpful, or uncomfortable?
- Do you have any certifications/training you'd want shown?
- A "Verified" badge that signals top-tier providers — would that motivate you? What would you
  expect to do to earn it?

## 7. Pains & wishes
- What's the most annoying / time-consuming part of running your business?
- If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about how you work, what would it be?
- What would make you look more professional to clients?

## 8. Willingness to pay (gently)
- Do you currently pay for any tools/subscriptions for your work? How much?
- If an app handled your bookings, reminders, payments and a professional profile — and you kept
  100% of what you earn — what would feel fair to pay monthly?
- How do you feel about platforms that take a % of each booking (like Home Salon's deposit cut)?

## 9. Tech comfort & logistics
- What phone do you use? Are you comfortable with apps?
- Do you have reliable data/wifi while working?
- Would your clients be comfortable booking via a link you send them?

## 10. Wrap-up
- Would you be open to being our first test provider — we set up your profile and you try it with
  real clients, free, and tell us what works?
- Anything I didn't ask that I should have?

---

### What we're listening for (analysis notes)
- **Biggest pain** → confirms which feature to lead with (likely no-shows/admin or trust).
- **Payment behaviour** → validates deposit + which methods to prioritise.
- **Trust concerns** → validates the profile/verification emphasis.
- **Willingness to pay** → sets the individual-tier price.
- **Discovery reality** → confirms the "provider brings own clients" (Phase 1) assumption.
- **Any discomfort** with photos/reviews/ID → shapes how we present the trust layer.
